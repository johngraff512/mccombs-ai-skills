# Discipline Guide — McCombs Case Toolkit

This reference helps Claude adapt case toolkit outputs across all McCombs departments.
Every skill in the toolkit works across disciplines. When gathering inputs, always ask
about the instructor's **department and course** so outputs use appropriate frameworks,
language, and examples.

---

## Departments and Common Frameworks

### Strategy / Management
- Porter's Five Forces, VRIO, Value Chain Analysis
- BCG Growth-Share Matrix, GE-McKinsey Matrix
- Blue Ocean Strategy, Platform Economics
- Dynamic Capabilities, Transaction Cost Economics
- Generic Strategies (cost leadership, differentiation, focus)
- Game Theory, Stakeholder Analysis, SWOT

### Finance
- Discounted Cash Flow (DCF), Net Present Value (NPV)
- Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)
- Capital Structure Analysis (Modigliani-Miller, trade-off theory)
- Financial Ratio Analysis (liquidity, profitability, leverage)
- Comparable Company Analysis, Precedent Transactions
- Risk-Return Trade-offs, CAPM, Option Pricing
- Dividend Policy, Working Capital Management

### Marketing
- STP: Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning
- Marketing Mix (4Ps / 7Ps)
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Brand Equity Models (Keller, Aaker)
- Customer Journey Mapping, Jobs-to-Be-Done
- Pricing Strategy (cost-plus, value-based, dynamic)
- Diffusion of Innovation, Product Lifecycle

### Operations / Supply Chain
- Process Mapping and Flow Analysis
- Lean / Six Sigma, Theory of Constraints
- Capacity Planning and Utilization
- Supply Chain Design and Risk Management
- Quality Management (TQM, statistical process control)
- Inventory Models (EOQ, safety stock, JIT)
- Make vs. Buy Decisions

### Management Information Systems (MIS)
- System Architecture and Design
- Data Governance and Privacy Frameworks
- Cybersecurity Risk Assessment
- Technology Adoption Models (TAM, UTAUT)
- IT Portfolio Management
- Build vs. Buy vs. SaaS Decisions
- Digital Transformation Frameworks

### Accounting
- Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)
- Audit Risk Model (inherent, control, detection risk)
- Materiality Assessment
- Fair Value Measurement (ASC 820)
- Consolidation and Business Combinations
- Internal Controls (COSO framework)
- Ethical Decision-Making in Accounting

## How Discipline Affects Each Skill

### Case Generator
- **Dilemma framing:** Strategy cases frame dilemmas as competitive positioning choices.
  Finance cases frame them as capital allocation or valuation decisions. Marketing cases
  frame them as customer or brand choices. Operations cases frame them as efficiency vs.
  quality trade-offs. MIS cases frame them as technology choices.
- **Exhibits:** Strategy → competitive comparison tables, market share charts. Finance →
  income statements, cash flow projections, valuation multiples. Marketing → segmentation
  data, survey results, pricing grids. Operations → process diagrams, capacity data,
  quality metrics. Accounting → financial statements with line-item detail, ratio analysis.

### Teaching Note Generator
- **Discussion questions:** Should use discipline-appropriate language. Strategy asks
  "What is the source of competitive advantage?" Finance asks "How should we value this
  opportunity?" Marketing asks "Which customer segment should we target?" Operations asks
  "Where is the bottleneck?"
- **Board plan:** Columns should reflect the discipline's analytical arc. Strategy:
  Sources of Advantage → Competitive Threats → Strategic Options. Finance: Valuation
  Drivers → Risk Factors → Capital Allocation Options. Marketing: Customer Segments →
  Value Propositions → Go-to-Market Approaches.
- **Pre-class questions:** Should probe the discipline's core tensions, not default to
  competitive advantage.

### Class Exercise Generator
- **Exercise types work across all disciplines.** A Think-Pair-Share works for "What is
  the source of competitive advantage?" (strategy) just as well as "What discount rate
  should we use?" (finance) or "Which customer segment is most attractive?" (marketing).
- **Framework Application exercises** should use the discipline's frameworks, not default
  to Five Forces.
- **Debates** should argue discipline-appropriate positions: strategy debates strategic
  paths, finance debates capital structure choices, marketing debates pricing strategies.

### Case Idea Generator
- **Framework fit** should evaluate against the discipline's frameworks, not assume
  strategy frameworks are universal. A good finance case idea maps to capital structure
  or valuation frameworks. A good marketing case idea maps to segmentation or positioning.
- **Teachable moment** is discipline-specific: strategy cases teach about competitive
  dynamics, finance cases teach about risk and return, marketing cases teach about
  customer value.

### Slide Generator
- **Framework slides** should render whatever framework the discipline requires. The
  Five Forces diagram is one option; a DCF waterfall, positioning map, or process flow
  are equally valid.
- **Discussion question slides** should use the discipline's language.
- **Data slides** should show discipline-appropriate metrics: revenue and market share
  for strategy; margins and ROIC for finance; CAC and CLV for marketing; utilization
  rates for operations.

### Case Refresher
- **Materiality thresholds** vary by discipline. A 20% revenue change matters for
  strategy; a credit rating downgrade matters for finance; a customer preference shift
  matters for marketing; a supply chain disruption matters for operations.
- **Update categories** should reflect the discipline: strategy uses Competitive,
  Strategic, Financial, Regulatory. Finance might use Valuation, Capital Markets,
  Regulatory, Credit. Marketing might use Customer, Competitive, Brand, Channel.
